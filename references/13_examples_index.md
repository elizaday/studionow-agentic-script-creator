# Examples Index

Use these as thinking references, not templates to copy.

## Example types to learn from

### A. AI heritage / cinematic thesis pieces
What to learn:
- visual philosophy
- execution discipline
- what the film is and is not
- restraint in AI language
- continuity rules across time

Use when:
- the brief needs mood, thesis, and visual logic
- the output is not an explainer
- AI is part of the method, not the story

Watch for:
- beautiful abstraction with no assignment clarity

### B. Modular city / localization scripts
What to learn:
- what stays fixed
- what swaps per market
- how to write brackets cleanly
- how to scale one script into many versions
- how to root localization in behavior, not just landmarks

Use when:
- city, market, or retail versions are required

### C. Internal business case / award scripts
What to learn:
- contrast
- chapter logic
- proof structure
- testimonial placement
- emotional control with metrics

Use when:
- the work already happened and needs to be proven

### D. Platform / system explainers
What to learn:
- problem/solution logic
- UI and graphic writing
- clarity under technical subject matter
- scale and rollout storytelling

Use when:
- introducing tools, systems, models, or internal capabilities

### E. Brand platform launch / audience definition scripts
What to learn:
- supers as stacked argument toward a platform reveal
- naming audience segments as characters, not demographics ("The Self-Manifestors", "Achievers", "Adventurers")
- how to build through sub-audiences before naming the whole
- earned platform climax: the campaign name arrives last, after the logic is complete
- short declarative supers that accumulate meaning across multiple beats

Use when:
- launching a new global or regional platform
- defining a cultural audience in a brand context
- the brief centers on "who this is for" as the central idea

### F. Recovery, comeback, and case study sizzle scripts
What to learn:
- three-beat arc: crash metric → pivot decision → proof outcome
- data as drama: the decline number is the scene, not the footnote
- "AND" before a triumph result as a structural signal
- proof delivered as declaration, not annotation ("AND BECAME #1 SPARKLING BRAND IN LATAM")
- chapter logic with super titles that name each phase of the story
- "WE DIDN'T JUST [expected result]. WE [actual result]." as a contrast move
- restraint in uplift: let the metric land, don't editorialize it

Use when:
- the story is one of recovery, growth, or transformation
- results are strong enough to speak for themselves
- the brief is for award submissions, internal business cases, or partner pitches

### G. Partnership sizzle scripts
What to learn:
- establish each brand's scale independently before the collision
- stats delivered as headlines, not footnotes: capitalized, owned, unhedged
- the shared audience bridge: what both brands' fans have in common is the idea
- two-part contrast supers for two-brand moments: "RUSH OF SPICY / HIT OF ICY"
- how to make a stat feel like a reveal, not a credential
- the partnership as a cultural event, not a product SKU

Use when:
- two brand names are equal co-leads
- the brief needs to sell the idea of the partnership before any execution
- scale and cultural overlap are the key proof points

## Important reminder

Examples are there to teach judgment, not surface mimicry.
Do not reuse their cadence blindly.
Take the structural intelligence, not the skin.
